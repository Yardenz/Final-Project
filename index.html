An Exploration of Infrastructure Legacy: Salt Lake City 2002 Winter Olympic Games


Urban Legacy:

Salt Lake City was the host of the 2002 Winter Olympic Games from Feb. 8 - 24. The large scale investment, bidding, and preparation for the Olympic games as well as the impact on the urban environment after the games leave town results in unique effects. This map explores infrastructure projects built or renovated for use during the Olympics, how the sights changed in preparation for the Olympics, and what the sights are today.

Public and Private Investment:

Each site contains information on public or private entities that invested in these development projects and the amount of funding from each source.

Investment Sources:

  • SLOC : the Salt Lake City Olympic Organizing Committee
  • Other : a mix of state and local government and private monies
  • Federal : funds from the U.S federal government

Explore the map by clicking building outlines and scrolling through a pop-up window that contains information describing select venues. Information on the venues includes a combination of satellite imagery, web photographs, and sources of investment in the sight in preparation for the Olympics.
